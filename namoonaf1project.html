Namoona F1 Project

Namoona F1 Project is an Indian motorsport engineering initiative established by Namoona Enterprises Private Limited. The project focuses on Formula-style vehicle development, motorsport engineering education, advanced manufacturing, simulation technologies and race-car research.

The initiative forms part of the long-term technology roadmap of Namoona Enterprises and seeks to develop capabilities in vehicle design, aerodynamics, manufacturing, motorsport operations and engineering management. Formula One is regarded as a long-term strategic aspiration rather than a confirmed entry programme.

History

The Namoona F1 Project was established in 2024 as a dedicated motorsport division of Namoona Enterprises. The programme was conceived to develop indigenous motorsport engineering capabilities through progressive vehicle development and applied engineering research.

The initiative originated from educational motorsport models and evolved into a roadmap that includes radio-controlled platforms, full-scale Formula-style chassis projects and advanced technology demonstrators.

Mission

To advance motorsport engineering, vehicle technology and racing innovation through education, research and practical vehicle development programmes.

Objectives

  • Develop indigenous motorsport engineering capabilities.
  • Promote Formula-style vehicle design and manufacturing.
  • Create engineering training platforms.
  • Advance research in aerodynamics and vehicle dynamics.
  • Support STEM education through motorsport.
  • Build long-term capabilities for international motorsport participation.

Vehicle Development Programme

NM07

A 1:25 scale fully 3D-printable Formula-style engineering model developed by Namoona 3D Labs. It served as an educational platform for motorsport design and additive manufacturing.

NM08

A 1:20 scale radio-controlled Formula-style engineering vehicle developed as a dynamic testing platform for steering, suspension and electronics integration.

NM22

An experimental motorsport concept used to investigate advanced race-car design methodologies and packaging concepts.

NM26

The first planned full-scale Formula-style chassis under the programme. The vehicle is being developed in collaboration with the Aeolus Racing Team of IIT Hyderabad.

  • Vehicle Type: Full-scale prototype
  • Planned Engine: Yamaha R15
  • Purpose: Motorsport engineering training platform
  • Status: In development

IN-1947

A conceptual motorsport vehicle inspired by Indian engineering heritage.

Flair16-GTE

A grand touring endurance racing concept vehicle developed as part of the programme's exploration of endurance motorsport.

NM29

The NM29 is the planned final prototype stage in the current roadmap and is envisioned as the most advanced engineering demonstrator developed under the programme.

  • Vehicle Type: Advanced Prototype
  • Role: Advanced Motorsport Technology Demonstrator
  • Status: Planned

Partnerships and Collaborations

Aeolus Racing Team, IIT Hyderabad

The Namoona F1 Project collaborates with the Aeolus Racing Team of IIT Hyderabad on Formula-style race-car development, vehicle dynamics studies, manufacturing methodologies and motorsport engineering education.

The collaboration supports the NM26 chassis programme and broader knowledge exchange in motorsport systems engineering.

Research and Innovation

Research activities focus on aerodynamics, lightweight structures, motorsport simulation, artificial intelligence applications, digital engineering workflows and advanced manufacturing technologies.

Educational Initiatives

The project supports engineering education through Formula-style vehicle programmes, student mentorship, engineering workshops, internships and collaborative research activities.

Long-Term Vision

The Namoona F1 Project forms part of Namoona Enterprises' broader roadmap extending toward 2047.

Through successive vehicle programmes, engineering collaborations, simulation capabilities and manufacturing development, the initiative seeks to establish a sustainable motorsport engineering ecosystem in India.

Its long-term vision includes participation in international motorsport categories and the development of technologies relevant to top-level racing competition.
