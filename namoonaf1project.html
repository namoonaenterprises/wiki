Namoona F1 Project

Namoona F1 Project is an Indian motorsport engineering initiative established by Namoona Enterprises Private Limited with the objective of developing the technological, financial, and organizational capabilities required to enter the FIA Formula One World Championship as a constructor by the 2029 season.

The project focuses on advanced race car engineering, simulation technologies, aerodynamics, data analytics, manufacturing systems, and motorsport innovation. If successful, the initiative aims to become India's first privately owned Formula One constructor.

History

The Namoona F1 Project was established in 2024 as a dedicated motorsport division of Namoona Enterprises Private Limited.

The initiative was founded by mechanical engineer and entrepreneur Angajala Nikhil Chaitanya, who envisioned the creation of a globally competitive Indian Formula One constructor.

The project was launched to address the absence of an Indian-owned Formula One team and to promote the development of advanced motorsport technologies within India.

Mission

To establish India as a competitive force in Formula One through engineering excellence, technological innovation, and sustainable motorsport development.

The project seeks to develop indigenous expertise in race car design, simulation, manufacturing, and motorsport operations.

Objectives

  • Achieve Formula One constructor status by 2029.
  • Develop indigenous motorsport technologies.
  • Create a pipeline of Indian motorsport engineers.
  • Build expertise in Formula One aerodynamics.
  • Advance AI-assisted engineering methodologies.
  • Promote innovation-led engineering education.

Vehicle Development Programme

The Namoona F1 Project follows a phased vehicle development strategy intended to progress from educational engineering models to full-scale race car prototypes. Each vehicle in the programme is designated with the prefix NM (Namoona Motorsport) followed by a project number.

Namoona NM07

The Namoona NM07 was the first vehicle developed under the Namoona F1 Project. Introduced as a 1:25 scale engineering demonstrator, the vehicle was designed as a fully 3D-printable Formula One-inspired model intended for education, design validation, and public outreach.

  • Scale: 1:25
  • Manufacturer: Namoona 3D Labs
  • Construction: Fully 3D Printable
  • Purpose: Educational and Concept Validation Platform
  • Development Phase: Completed

The NM07 served as the project's first demonstration of aerodynamic styling, modular design methodologies, and additive manufacturing workflows.

Namoona NM08

The Namoona NM08 is a 1:20 scale radio-controlled engineering model developed as the successor to the NM07. Unlike its predecessor, the NM08 was designed to function as a dynamic testing platform capable of evaluating vehicle behavior, steering systems, suspension concepts, and remote-control integration.

  • Scale: 1:20
  • Configuration: Radio Controlled
  • Manufacturer: Namoona 3D Labs
  • Construction: Fully 3D Printable
  • Purpose: Dynamic Testing and Vehicle Development
  • Development Phase: Ongoing

The NM08 represents the project's transition from static engineering models toward operational motorsport development platforms.

Namoona NM26

The Namoona NM26 is the first full-scale vehicle being developed under the Namoona F1 Project. The vehicle is being designed in collaboration with the Aeolus Racing Team of IIT Hyderabad and serves as the programme's initial full-scale motorsport engineering platform.

The NM26 is intended to resemble a modern Formula One car while maintaining a budget-conscious architecture suitable for engineering education, driver training, and motorsport research.

  • Vehicle Type: Full-Scale Prototype
  • Power Unit: Yamaha R15 Engine (Planned)
  • Development Partner: Aeolus Racing Team, IIT Hyderabad
  • Purpose: Motorsport Engineering Training Platform
  • Primary Goal: Affordable Formula-Style Race Car Development
  • Development Phase: In Development

The project aims to provide practical experience in chassis design, manufacturing, aerodynamics, vehicle dynamics, race engineering, and motorsport operations. The NM26 is expected to serve as a training platform for future members of the Namoona F1 Project.

Namoona NM29

The Namoona NM29 is the planned final prototype phase before a proposed Formula One constructor entry programme. The vehicle is envisioned as the most advanced development platform within the Namoona F1 Project roadmap.

The NM29 is expected to integrate lessons learned from previous development vehicles while incorporating advanced aerodynamic systems, lightweight structures, simulation-driven design methodologies, and modern motorsport engineering practices.

  • Vehicle Type: Advanced Prototype
  • Target Completion: 2029
  • Purpose: Final Technology Demonstrator
  • Role: Formula One Entry Preparation Platform
  • Status: Planned

The NM29 is intended to validate the technical capabilities, organizational structure, and engineering processes required for a future Formula One constructor programme.

Formula One Entry Program

The long-term objective of the Namoona F1 Project is participation in the FIA Formula One World Championship as a constructor.

The project intends to submit an official Formula One constructor application and compete as the championship's twelfth constructor beginning with the 2029 Formula One season.

Research and Innovation

Research activities focus on advanced aerodynamics, lightweight structures, motorsport simulation, artificial intelligence applications, digital engineering workflows, and advanced manufacturing technologies.

Educational Initiatives

The project seeks to support engineering education through student motorsport programs, Formula Student mentorship, engineering workshops, internships, and collaborative research projects.

Future Plans

  • Establish a dedicated motorsport engineering center.
  • Develop proprietary race car technologies.
  • Create advanced simulation laboratories.
  • Recruit international motorsport talent.
  • Participate in feeder racing categories.
  • Pursue Formula One constructor approval.
